Top 10 National Park FAQ's

1. How many national parks are there in the United States?
There are 63 national parks in the US.
2. What are the most famous parks in the US?
The Grand Canyon, Yosemite, Yellowstone, and the Rocky Mountain are a few of the most visited national parks in the Western United States. On the Eastern United States the most visited parks are Acadia, Great Smoky Mountains and the Everglades.
3. Do national parks have guided tours?
Yes. Every national park offers tours that may be guided by the national park service or by certified tour guides.
4. Can I drive into a national park
Yes. Most national parks in the United States are accessible by car(RV or bus).
5. Do I need to pay to enter
Yes. Each national park has their own set of rules/fees but you can expect to pay between $20-40 on average per vehicle. Depending on the park and the time of year you maybe able to obtain parking passes that are valid for several days at a time.
6. Do national parks have guided tours?
Yes. Every national park offers tours that may be guided by the national park service or by certified tour guides.
7. When were national parks established
In 1916 the National Park Service was established by congress to give americans the opportunity to enjoy public lands and make recreational activities more accessible to the public.
8. Is there an entity in charge of the national parks?
Yes. All national parks in the United States are managed and supervised by the National Park Service.
9. Are national parks open all year?
The answer depends on the park. Most national parks are open year-round, however you may find that some entrances are closed, due to weather most commonly. The best way to find out about specific parks will be on the NPS website.
10. Will I see any animals at the parks?
There are wildlife present at all national parks. To see the most wildlife national parks that cover large forest areas are those you should visit. Yellowstone National Park in Wyoming is a great park to see lots of wildlife.
